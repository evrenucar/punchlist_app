Mobile behavior guide

How each phone gesture behaves right now, one card at a time. Type in the box under any behavior you want changed, then send it all to the chat with one button. Empty boxes are ignored. Notes survive reloads on this machine.

Selecting and editing

tap

Tap a row to select it

A single tap anywhere on a row selects that row, including the empty space beside the text. Action buttons ride the selected row. Tapping a different row moves the selection.

tap the text

Tap into the text to edit

Tapping directly on a row's text opens the keyboard with the caret placed where you tapped. Normal word-selection and the caret behave like any text field.

Moving and reordering

hold ~0.5s, drag

Hold to move a row

Press a row anywhere (not just a grip) and hold about half a second until it lifts, then drag up or down. A drop indicator follows your finger; release drops it there. Dragging to the top or bottom edge auto-scrolls so you can cross groups.

hold past 1.5s

Hold longer to multi-select

Keep holding a still row past 1.5 seconds and the lift drops, the row joins the selection instead. This is how you start grabbing several rows at once with one finger.

sweep

Sweep to add rows to the selection

After a row joins the selection, sweep the finger up or down across more rows without releasing. Every crossed row joins, even on a fast sweep, and sweeping to the screen edge auto-scrolls. Hold another unrelated row past 1.5 seconds to add it too. A plain tap collapses back to one row.

Indenting

swipe sideways

Swipe right to indent, left to outdent

Swipe a row slowly sideways. It slides in steps (detents); release indents one level per step, up to three. Swipe a nested row left to outdent. The whole page never pans sideways while you do this.

Toggles and keys

tap

Chevrons and checkboxes

Tapping a chevron collapses or expands; tapping a checkbox completes or reopens. These fire on a plain tap with no accidental move or selection, even when tapped quickly in a row.

Enter

Enter at the start of a line

With the caret at the very start of a row that has text, Enter drops a fresh empty line above it at the same indent and puts the caret there. Your text is never split. A fully empty line is the exception: Enter there makes a new item below.

Groups and interference

hold, drag

Group headers move on touch too

A group header can be held and dragged like a row. Dropping a task on a group header lands it first inside that group rather than last.

nothing pops up

No native interference

Holding a row's text without tapping into it first raises no iOS magnifier, no copy/paste callout, and no context menu; the hold arms move or select instead. The browser's own long-press drag is suppressed on touch. Vertical scrolling still feels native.
